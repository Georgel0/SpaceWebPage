General Information

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and the second smallest planet in the Solar System, with a diameter about half that of Earth. It is often called the "Red Planet" due to the prevalent iron oxide on its surface, which gives it a distinctive reddish hue.

Mars's atmosphere is thin, composed mainly of carbon dioxide (95%), nitrogen (2.7%), and argon (1.6%). The average surface temperature is about -63Â°C, varying significantly between day and night and between the equator and the poles. The Martian surface is marked by impact craters, massive volcanoes, valleys, and polar ice caps made of water ice and carbon dioxide.

Recent research has focused on searching for signs of life on Mars, particularly by studying the presence of water. Although current conditions are not conducive to life as we know it, it is believed that Mars once had a warmer and wetter environment, with oceans and rivers.
