General Information

The Sun is the star at the center of the Solar System and the most important celestial body for life on Earth. It is a G-type main-sequence star, a "yellow dwarf," although its actual color is white. With a diameter of about 1.39 million kilometers, the Sun is approximately 109 times larger than Earth and contains over 99.8% of the total mass of the Solar System.

The Sun's composition is predominantly hydrogen (about 73% of its mass, 92% of its volume) and helium (about 25% of its mass, 7% of its volume). The Sun's energy is generated by nuclear fusion in its core, where hydrogen atoms combine to form helium, releasing a huge amount of energy in the form of light and heat.

Solar activity includes sunspots, solar flares, and coronal mass ejections, which can influence space weather conditions around Earth, leading to auroras (borealis and australis) and, occasionally, disruptions in communications.
